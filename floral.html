Flower Symbolism

Flowers might seem like something that is not too relevant, but when you dig deeper into their history
you will understand how long and complicated the history of a flower symbolism is.
Whenever people didn't have enough courage to say something, they would look for perfect ways to express their emotions without speaking.

  1. Carnation

    2. This flower can appear in many different colours, such as red, purple and pink among others.
    It is important to know that each of these colours has its own meaning and symbolism.
    In a general sense, a carnation is a symbol of love.
    But, if we take into account the colour in which we have seen a carnation, we can talk about
    a difference in the meanings of these flowers.

  2. Daisy

    3. This flower has always been a powerful symbol of innocence and purity
    And I look at this simple but yet so impressive plant automatically returns us to childhood, remembers
    the favourite people of that period, the carelessness and warmth of the parental home.
    In addition to all of the above, in this flower,the strongest image is persistance, perserverance and unobtrusive powerful

  3. Sunflower

    4. This has been considered a symbol of Joy and Life, but also a symbol of loyalty and longevity.
    This flower radiates passion and positive energy, and turns its flowers and petals to the sun everyday.
    Sunflower comes only in one colour, but this colour is enough to express the strong symbolical meaning of this beautiful flower.

  4. Dahlia

    5. This flower is one of the most beautiful flowers in the world. There's something special to it that can only be described
    by looking at the Dahlia Flower.It signifies commitment to another person and eternal love.
    People would gift Dahlia Flowers to their partners in order to show their deep appreciation and love to keep
    their love flame burning.

  5. Peony

    6. Peony is a gorgeous scented flower that has been adorned with gardens since ancient times.
    The message it gives is to stay honorable and respectful towards others because our actions affect other people.
    You should always keep your head up high and act toward others the way you want to be treated. The Peony Flower is
    also telling you to take care of your inner and outer beauty, and to always treat everyday like it is the most special day of your life.

  6. Lily

    7. Lily is a symbol of motherhood, as well as rebirth. Because of this, a bouquet of lilies could be the perfect
    gift to give to your mother or your grandmother. Another meaning related to lilies is love. And because of this, Lilies are
    used on weddings. It is believed that it can be a symbol of good luck as well.
